About me

I'm a recent Applied Mathematics graduate, specialising in computer science. I'm passionate about the intersection of Mathematics and Computing, namely Modelling and Programming. I believe that this field is instrumental to help solve the problems of our time.

Started my professional journey as an Engineering Apprentice at BAE Systems. This experience served as a catalyst for nurturing my affinity for computational systems, exposing me to a plethora of practical applications within Computational Mathematics. Collaborating with highly skilled teams engaged in this domain, I was actively involved in resolving challenges, witnessing the true impact of that this field has to offer.

I was motivated to pursue Applied Mathematics with a specific focus on its applications in computer science. This academic pursuit allowed me to refine both my theoretical knowledge and practical skills. Alongside gaining a solid foundation in theoretical mathematics, I expanded my comprehension of computer systems.

For my bachelor's thesis, I find myself immersed in field of Mathematical Modelling, particularly analysing the Turing bifurcation phenomena occurring in reaction-diffusion models, using numerical methods. Engaging in research, developing ideas, and formulating theorems have brought me immense satisfaction. I have also had the privilege of engaging in thought-provoking discussions on these matters with my supervisor D.Avitabile.
