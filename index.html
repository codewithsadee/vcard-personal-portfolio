About me

Hi! My name is Eric Wang. I'm an undergraduate student who now is receiving all-rounded training in Cognitive Neuroscience. I am currently pursuing a Bachelor's degree Majoring in Cognitive Science at the Department of Psychology at the Chinese University of Hong Kong. This is fully funded by the University and the HK education bureau. In addition to that, I am also pursuing a Bachelor's degree in Computer Science and Mathematics via distance at the Massey University.

Basically, I am interested in every topic that lies at the intersection of human intelligence and artificial intelligence. This is indeed a big topic so I plan to narrow it down at some point in the future. There are several possible directions:

Theme 1:Brain computer interface (BCI)

Theme 2: VR and Cognition, and how can we build a virtual environment that appears to be more real

Theme 3: Brain-inspired algorithm that reaches like-human level cognition.

Whatever direction I eventually choose, there is no doubt that I will also keep in mind of the social responsibility of being a researcher(not yet but trying to). My future research will keep the alignment with social interests and industrial interests.

I already have a brief idea for some of the topics. But for others, my knowledge is close to 0. Therefore, my main focus now is to keep learning and build a solid foundation. While keep reaching out for research internship opportunities as if this is a mini "lab rotation". Do trust me, I am a hardworking, well-organised student and would make the most of my time at anyone's lab.

Approaches i take

  • Behavioral data

    Behavioral data are as important as the neuron connections in the brain. Through careful analysis of the tasks and the behavior elicited by it, we can understand the underlying mechanism of brain at algorithmic level.

  • Computational models

    Connectionist models and statistical models can help to develop complementary understanding and bridge the gap between human intelligence and artificial intelligence.

  • Brain imaging

    One of, if not, the most powerful tool in Cognitive Neuroscience. By recording and analysing neurological data through EEG and fMRI, we can learn how living brain execute certain cognitive functions in real-time. (implementation level)

  • Other New Technologies

    In this fast-developing world, new technologies emerge. Providing new ways of doing research and investigating human cognition. Some examples like VR and AR. I also have a great interest in using those in my future research projects
